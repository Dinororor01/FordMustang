Founder

Lee Iacocca

The man behind the idea and the dream, Lee Iacocca a son of an Italian immigrant family was an aspiring engineer who joined the Ford company when he was only 22 years old. After graduating from Lehigh with a degree in industrial engineering, quickly rising to the top, the young Mr. Iacocca quickly rose through the ranks of the company and changed the automobile business forever. Among a few “founding fathers”, Iacocca helped start up the early development of the Ford Mustang, which remains one of Ford’s most popular cars after over a half-century.

Following several successful campaigns, Iacocca found his true place in the product development department. After many concept car designs and bouncing ideas off his team, Iacocca initiated a worldwide competition to find the ideal model for what would eventually become the Ford Mustang. Amid the heated contest, Iacocca stumbled upon a sketch by Ford’s own Gale Halderman, and the design for the Ford Mustang was born. With the goal in mind of creating a “youth car” that would appeal to the youth of his time, he and his team set off on their journey to perfect the design of the famous and iconic Ford Mustang.
